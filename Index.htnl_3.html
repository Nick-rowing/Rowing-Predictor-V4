Where the numbers come from.

Recruiting standards are aggregated from publicly stated guidelines by collegiate programs, recruiting services, and USRowing selection documents. Programs publish ranges, not hard cutoffs — what's shown here is the consensus across reputable sources.

  • Sparks Consulting — collegiate rowing recruiting standards by program tier, published 2024-2026.
  • NCSA Sports — men's college rowing recruiting time guides, current cycle.
  • USRowing — U19 Selection & Development Camp criteria, U23 selection guidelines, National Team Testing protocols.
  • Crimson Education Athletics — published D1 rowing recruitment standards by weight class.
  • Harvard Lightweight Rowing — publicly stated recruiting thresholds (sub-6:30 2k, sub-21:00 6k).
  • Concept2 — published age-group standards and the Logbook ranking database.
  • Erg-to-water adjustments — coaching heuristics referencing Dr. Valery Kleshnev's BioRow biomechanics research.
Recruiting benchmarks are directional ranges, not guarantees. Programs evaluate trajectory, race results, height, weight, technique, academics, and character alongside erg scores. Standards shift year to year as the sport evolves — treat the numbers here as a starting reference, then verify against your target program's published criteria. On-water predictions are heuristic estimates that don't account for technique, crew compatibility, rigging, or weather.
